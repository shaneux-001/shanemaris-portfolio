Southwest's no-change-fee policy is one of its most distinctive customer commitments — but the experience of actually changing or canceling a flight had historically not lived up to that promise at the interface level. The redesign of the change and cancel experience aimed to bring the UX into alignment with the brand value: making it genuinely easy to adapt when travel plans shift.

## Context

Change and cancel flows are inherently high-stress interactions. Customers arrive at them when something has gone wrong — a schedule change, an unexpected conflict, a disruption. The design job is to reduce friction, communicate clearly, and give people confidence that they understand what will happen before they confirm any action. The existing flow had accumulated complexity over time and didn't consistently achieve those goals.

## The redesign

The work focused on information clarity at the decision points in the flow — making the cost and outcome of each choice legible before the customer commits, reducing the number of steps required to complete common actions, and ensuring the experience worked reliably across mobile and desktop viewports.

## Outcome

The redesigned flow reduced ambiguity at the most common friction points in the change and cancel experience, bringing the interface into closer alignment with Southwest's brand promise of customer-friendly flexibility.
