The native app homepage redesign represented a significant expansion of the Heart Design System's reach — from web into Southwest's iOS and Android apps. Moving a design system from web into native is not a straight translation. Component behavior, platform conventions, and performance constraints all differ, and this project was the first place the HDS team and native app teams had to work through those differences at scale.

## Context

Southwest's native apps carry a substantial share of the company's digital bookings and are the primary touchpoint for travelers on the day of travel. The homepage is the first thing members see when they open the app — a high-stakes surface. Before this project, the app homepage operated largely independent of HDS, with its own visual decisions and component patterns that had developed separately from the web system.

## The challenge of native translation

Design systems built for the web don't port directly to native. Interaction patterns that feel natural on a browser — hover states, cursor feedback, certain layout constructs — don't map to touch. At the same time, each platform (iOS, Android) has its own HIG guidelines and user expectations. Part of my work on this project was navigating that translation: what from HDS could be adopted directly, what needed platform-specific adaptation, and what needed to be invented from scratch as a new native pattern.

## Outcome

The native app homepage redesign established HDS's footprint in the native product and gave the system team its first production-tested set of native patterns. It also created a working model for how future native projects at Southwest could adopt the system — a playbook that subsequent teams have built from.
