The My Account section of southwest.com is where Rapid Rewards members manage their loyalty relationship with the airline — points balances, travel history, credit card status, and account preferences. The redesign was one of Heart DS's first major real-world adoptions, and it became the proving ground for a sustained A/B testing program that meaningfully improved credit card acquisition through the account experience.

## Context

My Account had historically been one of the most fragmented areas of the site — a collection of pages that had grown organically over time, each with its own visual logic, with limited coherence across the section. It was also one of the most strategically important: logged-in members are Southwest's highest-value customers, and the My Account experience directly influences engagement, satisfaction, and financial product uptake.

## First major HDS consumer

This project was among the first to adopt Heart DS at the component level in production. That meant making real decisions about how the system translated to a logged-in, data-dense context — member dashboards, account tables, status indicators, and dynamic content blocks that the system's initial component set wasn't fully designed for. The work surfaced a number of meaningful HDS gaps that were subsequently closed.

## A/B testing and credit card acquisition

Beyond the design overhaul, I led a series of A/B testing efforts within the account section focused on the Southwest Rapid Rewards® credit card. Through iterative testing — placement, messaging hierarchy, call-to-action framing — the team identified combinations that drove measurable improvements in card acquisition rates among logged-in members. The account section is a high-intent context when it comes to financial products: members who are actively engaging with their loyalty account are already invested in the Southwest relationship.

## Outcome

The redesigned My Account experience established a more coherent, system-backed foundation for the section, reduced visual fragmentation, and delivered a measurable lift in credit card acquisitions through the tested interface changes. The project also reinforced an important principle: adopting a design system and running structured experiments aren't separate initiatives — done together, they compound.
