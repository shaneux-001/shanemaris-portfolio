Irregular operations — weather delays, cancellations, aircraft swaps — are an unavoidable reality in commercial aviation. The reaccomm (reaccommodation) experience is Southwest's answer to disruption: a digital flow that helps affected customers understand their options and rebook quickly. The Enhanced Reaccom project was an effort to make that experience meaningfully better at the moments when it matters most.

## Context

When a flight is disrupted, customers are anxious and time-constrained. They need to understand their options quickly, make a decision with confidence, and complete the rebooking without additional friction. The existing reaccom experience had served its purpose but hadn't kept pace with the complexity of scenarios customers actually encountered, or with the capabilities the airline had developed to support them.

## The work

The redesign addressed the clarity and completeness of the option-presentation step — ensuring customers could see their alternatives, understand the trade-offs, and select a path without ambiguity. It also improved the flow's handling of edge cases: customers with complex itineraries, multiple passengers, or specific needs that the previous flow didn't surface cleanly.

## Outcome

The enhanced experience gave disrupted customers a clearer, more complete picture of their options at the moment they needed it most — reducing uncertainty and call center load during irregular operations events.
