In 2014, Southwest Airlines undertook a significant brand refresh: new logo, new aircraft livery, new visual identity system. The company's digital presence needed to follow. The homepage redesign was part of a broader digital effort to bring southwest.com into alignment with the new brand — a project I contributed to as part of the UX team.

## The 2014 rebrand

Southwest's 2014 rebrand was a meaningful shift in the company's visual identity. The classic, multi-colored "canyon blue" livery gave way to a more streamlined look anchored by a new heart logo — a design that leaned into the brand's warmth and approachability while modernizing its look. The digital team's job was to translate that new identity into an experience that felt intentional and cohesive, not just a new coat of paint on the old site.

## My contribution

Working as part of the UX team, I contributed to the design and validation of the new homepage experience — a surface that needed to serve a wide range of customer intents while expressing the refreshed brand accurately. The work involved close collaboration with brand and marketing partners to ensure the digital expression of the new identity felt right, as well as standard UX process work: research synthesis, wireframing, iteration, and handoff.

## Significance

This project was an early example of large-scale digital brand alignment at Southwest — bringing the web experience into sync with a company-wide rebrand rather than letting digital drift independently. It also planted early seeds for the thinking that would eventually grow into a more systematic approach to design consistency — a thread that runs forward through Heart DS.
